So you've managed to [setup Pure Comments](/getting-started-with-pure-comments) and you've [customised it's theme](/theming-pure-comments) so it looks *beautiful* with your blog. Now you need to know how to actually manage comments.

If you've configured email notifications with Amazon SES (strongly advised), you will receive a notification when someone leaves a comment on one of your posts that looks like this:

![comment notification](/content/images/managing-comments-in-pure-comments/comment-notification.webp)

The bottom of the notification contains a link to go and moderate the comment, which after logging in will show in the pending comments section:

![pending comment](/content/images/managing-comments-in-pure-comments/pending-comment.webp)

From here you can expand the pending comment to view the whole thing and moderate it. You can **Publish** the comment, which will make it visible on your blog post, **Delete** the comment if it's spam, or **Reply** to the comment.

<p class="notice tip">Replying to the comment will also publish it, so you don't need to publish it, then reply.</p>

![pending comment 02](/content/images/managing-comments-in-pure-comments/pending-comment-02.webp)

Once a pending comment has been published, it will be moved to the **Published Comments** section of the dashboard.

## Managing replies

Replies to an existing comment will also appear in the **Pending Comments** section, so you can moderate them. Once a reply has been published, it will move to the **Published Comments** section, but will be nested within the conversation the reply is a part of.

Here's what a conversation looks like in Pure Blog:

![comment thread](/content/images/managing-comments-in-pure-comments/comment-thread.webp)

Within a comment thread you can delete the entire thread, delete specific replies, and reply to any comment in the thread.
